Introduction

The Mahabharata is an ancient Indian epic that tells the story of the struggle for power between two families, the Pandavas and the Kauravas. The epic encompasses profound philosophical and devotional discourses, such as the Bhagavad Gita, and many moral lessons through its complex characters and their journeys.

Adi Parva (The Beginning)

The Adi Parva introduces the ancestors of the Pandavas and Kauravas, including Bhishma and the kings of Hastinapura. It tells of their births, upbringing, and the establishment of their family ties, laying the foundation for the coming conflict.

Sabha Parva (The Assembly Hall)

This parva describes the royal court and the famous game of dice in which Yudhishthira loses everything, including his kingdom and brothers, to Duryodhana. The humiliation of Draupadi in the Kauravas' court leads to their exile for 13 years.

Vana Parva (The Book of the Forest)

During their exile in the forest, the Pandavas face many challenges. Arjuna embarks on a journey to obtain divine weapons, while other adventures and discussions on life and righteousness take place.

Virata Parva (The Book of Virata)

The Pandavas, in disguise, spend the final year of their exile in the kingdom of Virata. This section narrates their service to King Virata, their eventual revelation, and preparations for war.

Udyoga Parva (The Book of Effort)

This parva details the efforts made by both the Pandavas and the Kauravas to avoid war through diplomatic negotiations, which ultimately fail, leading to the preparations for the great battle.

Bhishma Parva (The Book of Bhishma)

Bhishma, the great general of the Kauravas, leads the Kaurava army in the first part of the war. This parva also includes the Bhagavad Gita, where Krishna imparts profound teachings to Arjuna on the battlefield.

Drona Parva (The Book of Drona)

Following Bhishma's fall, Drona becomes the leader of the Kaurava army. This parva describes Drona's tactics and strategies, his victories, and his eventual downfall.

Karna Parva (The Book of Karna)

Karna, the tragic hero, takes command of the Kaurava forces. The intense battles between Karna and Arjuna, the use of divine weapons, and Karna's eventual death are the central events of this parva.

Shalya Parva (The Book of Shalya)

Shalya becomes the leader of the Kaurava army after Karna's death. This parva narrates the final stages of the war and the deaths of key warriors like Shalya, Duryodhana, and others.

Sauptika Parva (The Book of Sleeping Warriors)

This parva describes the night massacre carried out by Ashwatthama, Kritavarma, and Kripacharya, who kill the sleeping Pandava soldiers in a final act of vengeance.

Stri Parva (The Book of Women)

This parva depicts the grief of the women of both sides as they mourn the loss of their sons, husbands, and fathers in the war.

Shanti Parva (The Book of Peace)

After the war, Yudhishthira becomes the king and Bhishma imparts lessons on governance, ethics, and duties to him while lying on his bed of arrows.

Anushasana Parva (The Book of Instructions)

This parva continues Bhishma's teachings to Yudhishthira on various aspects of dharma and duty, including topics like charity, conduct, and righteousness.

Ashvamedhika Parva (The Book of Horse Sacrifice)

After the war, Yudhishthira conducts the Ashvamedha Yajna to affirm his authority as the emperor. Arjuna leads the horse across various kingdoms, symbolizing Yudhishthira's sovereignty.

Mausala Parva (The Book of Clubs)

This parva narrates the tragic end of the Yadavas, Krishna's clan, who destroy each other in a brawl using clubs made of iron, fulfilling a curse that was placed upon them.

Mahaprasthanika Parva (The Book of the Great Journey)

The Pandavas, having completed their time on earth, decide to embark on a final journey to the Himalayas, renouncing the world and their attachments.

Swargarohanika Parva (The Book of the Ascension to Heaven)

This parva narrates the ascent of the Pandavas to heaven. Yudhishthira is the last to ascend, having passed all tests and remaining steadfast in his principles.
